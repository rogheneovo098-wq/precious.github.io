Welcome to My Neuroengineering Journey

What happens inside the brain when you think, learn, move, or remember? How can we use mathematics, engineering, programming, and data to understand one of the most complex systems known to humanity?

Join me as I begin my journey into the world of neuroengineering—the field where neuroscience meets engineering, computation, and technology.

My long-term goal is to specialize in neuroengineering at the PhD level. But before tackling advanced research, I believe in building a strong foundation. That means starting from the basics and working my way up, one concept at a time.

This won't be just a reading journey. It will be a hands-on exploration of neuroengineering through research, coding, simulations, and projects. Along the way, I'll be documenting what I learn, breaking down complex ideas into simpler explanations, implementing concepts in code, and sharing my work publicly on GitHub.

One of the major goals of this journey is to complete 50 projects that highlight different aspects of neuroengineering. From understanding how the brain works to building computational models and simulations, each project will be an opportunity to learn by doing.

I'm not approaching this as an expert teaching others. Instead, I'm documenting the process of becoming one—sharing the challenges, discoveries, mistakes, and lessons learned along the way. My hope is that these notes, projects, and explanations will be useful to anyone curious about the intersection of neuroscience, engineering, mathematics, and computing.

If you're interested in understanding the brain through code, data, and engineering, you're welcome to follow along.

The journey starts with the basics.
